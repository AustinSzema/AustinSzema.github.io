What could be the next experimental game platform?

Austin Szema

4/9/26

There are a lot of unique and weird platforms that exist in the game space. Here at the ETC we have: The Cavern, The Jam-O-Drum, The Space Bridge, Board.Fun, Tilt 5, Meta Quest 3, Apple Vision Pro, Playdate Console, Xbox, PS5, Nintendo Switch, iPhones, Android, Mac, Linux, and Windows PCs.

And yet, at the end of the day, a lot of these devices are just Linux (Android) or Windows computers. So what makes them so different and unique?

The primary components of a unique platform are: input, output, and performance. These are the three main categories that determine the affordances of the platform and dictate what type of games can be made for them. The way in which the device receives input largely determines how the user engages with the device. We primarily use our eyes and ears to obtain outputs, just in different shapes and forms. This brings up an interesting question of creating a platform or device that has an output related to small, touch, or taste. We do have haptic feedback gloves, and there is currently a Cavern project being made that uses an arduino with a scent diffuser for an output that targets the sense of smell, however these aren’t very common in most devices. I suppose the rumble feature on controllers, and the vibration on phones do add a sense of haptic feedback in terms of output for sense of touch.

An idea for a new platform would be a room where the entire floor is covered in a giant grid of tiny buttons and it tracks your foot position by seeing which buttons are being pressed and then it can project visual holograms. In this case, we don’t have to worry about wearing a clunky headset like the meta quest, or holding a controller. In this setup, your feet become the controller, which very few games actually do.

This would essentially just be a giant iPad touchscreen on the floor, with material that would be detectable by a touchscreen on the bottom of your feet. This could allow for a lot more physicality and exercise in games, without the difficulties of actual full body motion tracking. Imagine fruit ninja where the entire floor is the screen, and you are now the sword, needing to run around and cut the fruit, while also avoiding the bombs. Or you could be in a tower defense game, moving around and placing down your towers physically on the screen, while also avoiding bullets and enemies.
